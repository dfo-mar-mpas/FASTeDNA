**FASTeDNA: a field-deployable, adaptable eDNA workflow for rapid detection and real-time aquatic surveillance** 

# Abstract

Environmental DNA (eDNA) has emerged as a powerful tool for monitoring and surveillance of aquatic invasive species (AIS). However, conventional workflows rely on centralized laboratory processing which can introduce delays in result turnaround, limiting its effectiveness where time-sensitive decisions are required. Here, we introduce FASTeDNA, an  field-deployable, adaptable eDNA workflow for the rapid (4–5 hours) in situ detection of marine and freshwater AIS. This protocol supports point-of-need applications aimed at identifying and reducing introduction risk, including watercraft inspections at point of entry locations, vessel biofouling assessments, regulatory enforcement and remote monitoring. We developed and tested the protocol with field samples targeting high-risk marine (Ciona intestinalis, Botryllus schlosseri, Botrylloides violaceus) and freshwater (Dreissena polymorpha) AIS across a range of abundances, yielding accurate, reliable, and repeatable results comparable to those obtained through laboratory-based analyses. The protocol was subsequently evaluated by individuals with a range of molecular laboratory experience, all of whom produced accurate results and provided feedback to improve overall user-friendliness. Additional field trials conducted by end users not involved in the development confirmed the accuracy and broader applicability of the protocol and associated equipment across other species and contexts. These results demonstrate that FASTeDNA provides a rapid, reliable, and newly accessible tool for real-time aquatic surveillance. This approach enables improved decision-making for biosecurity, conservation, and environmental management in both marine and freshwater systems, complementing laboratory infrastructure while expanding capacity and improving accessibility.
